World Computer Exchange

Technology + Education = Potential

Our Mission:
To reduce the digital divide for youth in developing countries;
to use our global network of partnerships to enhance communities in these countries;
and to promote the reuse of electronic equipment and its ultimate disposal in an environmentally responsible manner.

What We Do

Our Services
Computers

WCE ships donated refurbished desktop and laptop computers to groups who choose to become our Partners. Partners can be NGOs, government ministries, schools, orphanages, libraries, youth centres, telecentres, universities & entrepreneurs.

Content

WCE prides itself on is the preloaded educational content installed in their computers, which covers all standard, grade school subjects, such as history, language, and science, as well as resources to help students learn basic programming skills and engineering methodologies.

Principles

By expanding access to computers and the Internet, we aspire to enhance the quality of education in the developing world and help increase the chances for youth, their families and community to overcome poverty, lead productive lives and contribute to the growth of their economies.

Read More >
Strategic Allies

Whether working with UNDP in Bangladesh, World Vision in Honduras, Rotary in Mali or Peace Corps volunteers in El Salvador, World Computer Exchange works through our 900 partner organisations in 79 developing countries to deliver on our mission.

Computers for Girls

We know that educated girls become educated women who have the skills, knowledge and opportunities to improve their lives and their communities. Yet this can only happen if the digital divide is bridged first.

By partnering with organizations and schools that support girls' education, WCE helps improve the chances for girls to get a 21st century education -- essential for alleviating poverty, and breaking down the social and political barriers that exclude them from fully participating in the future of their country.

Sustainable eWaste Initiative

The generation of electronic waste or e-waste and its negative impact on the environment and on living-beings is an issue with growing recognition in the international community.

The primary focus of the Initiative is to grow a network of WCE Chapters in developing countries. These Chapters will collect retired computers locally for refurbishment and reuse rather than import them. Contrary to common perception, domestic use of computers and electronics is creating a new tide of e-waste inside developing countries: in 2011 over 80 percent of e-waste generated in the U.S. was processed domestically, and less than 4 percent was sent overseas.
